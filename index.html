What's Inside

12 Agency Red Flags That Cost Childcare Directors Thousands

Here's a preview of what the checklist covers. These aren't opinions — they're patterns pulled from real conversations with childcare owners who learned these lessons the hard way.

1
They talk about leads — not enrollment An agency that reports on clicks and impressions but never mentions licensed capacity doesn't understand how childcare businesses actually make money.
2
Zero childcare-specific case studies If their portfolio is "education" or "local service businesses," they don't understand enrollment seasons, parent decision cycles, or why infant rooms fill differently than preschool rooms.
3
They push more ad spend when results are poor "You just need to increase your budget" is a red flag. When the right strategy isn't in place, more spend only amplifies the problem faster.
4
Long lock-in contracts with no enrollment benchmarks A 12-month retainer with no defined enrollment milestones means you're buying their time, not their results. You should never be locked in without a clear path to full capacity.
5
They can't explain their targeting methodology How they identify parents in your specific zip code who are actively searching for care — and how they exclude those who aren't ready — matters more than their creative.
6
No system for converting leads into tours Leads without a follow-up conversion system are just names in a spreadsheet. Ask what happens in the 48 hours after a parent submits a form. If they can't answer, walk away.
7
They use generic education templates Your website shouldn't look like a tutoring center or a private school. If their templates are interchangeable, your marketing will be invisible in your market.
8
Their reporting never shows cost per enrolled child The only metric that matters is how much you paid to fill one seat. An agency that can't calculate this has never been held accountable to your actual business goal.
9
They don't understand seasonal enrollment cycles Every childcare market has enrollment windows. An agency that treats August like February will waste your peak acquisition budget.
10
They've never filled a center before Running ads is not the same as filling a childcare center. Ask them specifically: "Tell me about a center you helped reach full capacity." If they hesitate, you have your answer.
